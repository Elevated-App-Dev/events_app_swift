# Real Estate Agent Simulator - Market Research

## Research Date: January 6, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

The real estate agent simulator niche on mobile is **underserved**. Existing games lean toward "tycoon" (investment/flipping) rather than authentic day-to-day agent work simulation. With 10+ years of real estate experience, there's an opportunity to create something more authentic than competitors.

---

## Existing Competitors

### Estate Agent Simulator
- **Platforms**: Mobile (iOS/Android) + Steam
- **Focus**: Buy/sell/rent properties, client negotiation, rent collection
- **Realism Level**: Medium
- **Steam Reviews**: 68% positive (mixed)
- **Status**: Early Access
- **Weakness**: Critics say it "doesn't have a satisfying gameplay loop"
- **Source**: [Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2553050/Estate_Agent_Simulator/)

### Real Estate Business Simulator
- **Platforms**: Android
- **Focus**: Career progression, client matching, renovations
- **Realism Level**: Medium
- **Release**: January 2025
- **Source**: [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.infinity.property.house.design.brothers.game&hl=en_US)

### Landlord Tycoon / Landlord Go
- **Platforms**: iOS/Android
- **Focus**: GPS-based, buy real-world properties
- **Realism Level**: Low - arcade/investment focused
- **Source**: [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/landlord-tycoon-business-games/id950949627)

### Real Estate Tycoon: Simulator
- **Platforms**: iOS
- **Focus**: Interactive storylines, negotiations
- **Realism Level**: Low
- **Issues**: Reported pay-to-progress problems
- **Source**: [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/real-estate-tycoon-simulator/id6477774274)

## Market Gap Analysis

### What Existing Games Focus On
- Investment/tycoon mechanics (buy low, sell high)
- Property flipping (renovate and sell)
- Landlord management (collect rent)
- Arcade-style gameplay

### What's MISSING from the Market
- Authentic day-to-day agent work simulation
- Realistic client relationship management with varied personalities
- Full transaction lifecycle (listing to closing)
- Lead generation and marketing strategies
- Dealing with inspections, appraisals, failed deals
- Commission-based income management
- Building a real estate business from scratch

## Competitive Differentiation

| Their Games | Potential Differentiation |
|-------------|---------------------------|
| Generic client types | Realistic buyer/seller personalities (first-time buyers, investors, relocators, divorce sales, estate sales) |
| Simple buy/sell | Full transaction lifecycle (listing presentation, pricing strategy, showings, offers, inspections, appraisal, closing) |

## Mobile vs Console Analysis

### Why Mobile First

| Factor | Mobile Advantage |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Target Audience** | Aspiring agents, curious learners - broader than "gamers" |
| **Session Length** | 5-15 min sessions fit real estate learning style |
| **Accessibility** | More people have phones than consoles |
| **Development Cost** | $0-5K vs $50K-500K+ for console |
| **Expectations** | Stylized graphics acceptable |
| **Discovery** | App Store optimization reaches non-gamers |

### Console Barriers
- Dev kit costs ($2,500-5,000+)
- Platform approval process (months)
- Unity Pro license required ($2,040/year)
- Higher production value expectations
- Smaller audience for simulation games

### Recommended Platform Path
```
Phase 1: Mobile (iOS/Android)
├── Validate concept with real players
├── Low cost, fast iteration
└── Build audience + revenue

Phase 2: PC/Steam (if mobile succeeds)
├── Enhanced features for enthusiasts
├── Gaming press coverage
└── Build credibility

Phase 3: Console (if PC succeeds)
├── Apply to ID@Xbox / PlayStation Partners
├── Port with enhancements
└── Or attract publisher interest
```

## Target Audience Profiles

### Primary: Aspiring Real Estate Agents
- **Who**: People considering a real estate career
- **Need**: Learn the job before committing to licensing
- **Platform**: Mobile (learning on commute)
- **Spending**: Willing to pay for education

### Secondary: Real Estate Curious
- **Who**: Homebuyers wanting to understand the process
- **Need**: Demystify buying/selling a home
- **Platform**: Mobile (casual interest)
- **Spending**: Free-to-play with optional IAP

### Tertiary: Simulation Enthusiasts
- **Who**: Fans of business/tycoon games
- **Need**: Deep, authentic simulation gameplay
- **Platform**: Mobile or PC
- **Spending**: Will pay upfront for quality

## Key Insights

1. **Market opportunity is strong** - No dominant "authentic agent simulator" exists

3. **Your experience is the moat** - 10 years of real knowledge vs. game designers guessing
4. **Mobile-first makes sense** - Target audience, session length, and cost all favor mobile
5. **Existing competitors have mixed reviews** - Room for quality improvement
